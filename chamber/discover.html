El Cusuco

The Cusuco National Park is a cloud forest located in the Merendón Mountains, northwest of San Pedro Sula.The forest is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The park is a popular destination for hikers and birdwatchers, and it offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. Visitors to El Cusuco can explore the park's trails, and its waterfalls

Archeological Park Currusté

Curusté is a small village located in the mountains near San Pedro Sula. The village is known for its traditional crafts, including pottery and weaving. Visitors to Curusté can watch local artisans at work, and purchase handmade souvenirs to take home. The village is also home to a number of small cafes and restaurants, where visitors can sample traditional Honduran cuisine.

El Merendón

El Merendón is the highest peak in the Merendón Mountains, and offers stunning views of the surrounding countryside. The mountain is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, jaguars, and howler monkeys. Visitors to El Merendón can hike to the summit, and enjoy panoramic views of the San Pedro Sula valley. The mountain is also home to a number of small villages, where visitors can experience traditional Honduran culture.

Tara National Park

Tara National Park is located in the mountains near San Pedro Sula. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including deer, jaguars, and howler monkeys. Visitors to Tara can explore the park's trails, and enjoy stunning views of the surrounding countryside.

Anthropology and History Museum

The Anthropology and History Museum is located in the heart of San Pedro Sula. The museum is home to a collection of artifacts from the region's pre-Columbian past, including pottery, tools, and jewelry. The museum also features exhibits on the history of the region, including the Spanish conquest and the development of the city of San Pedro Sula.

El Bazar

El Bazar is a traditional market located in the buildings of the Chamber of Commerce of San Pedro Sula, called "Expocentro". The market is opened on Saturdays, where locals and tourists alike, can buy a wide variety of goods,including clothing, jewelry, and handicrafts. Visitors to El Bazar can sample traditional Honduran street food, and enjoy live music and entertainment.
